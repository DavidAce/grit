# Commit Messages

Use a clear subject line and a short itemized body when the change needs explanation.

- Mention durable developer-facing facts.
- Describe the final state of the code, not the chronology of the work.
- Keep wording direct and concrete.
- Avoid vague or promotional language.
- Avoid overused metaphorical terms such as "wire", "harness", "pipeline", and "smoke".
- Do not include literal `\n` sequences in commit messages.
- Prefer concrete wording such as "save and load HDF5 files" over shorthand such as "HDF5 warm starts".
